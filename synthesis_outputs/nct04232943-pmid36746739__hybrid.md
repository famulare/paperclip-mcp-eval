The paper's central empirical claim, supported by web/API abstract and Paperclip's `map`-first-result for PMC9996288, is that **adding the dmLT mucosal adjuvant to intramuscularly administered IPV in IPV-primed healthy adults did not improve humoral or intestinal immunity and did not reduce fecal poliovirus shedding after a Day-29 bOPV challenge**, relative to IPV alone. ### Prompt 2 — Study design / methods

From the abstract and the Paperclip-grep methods snippet: phase 1, randomized, three-arm study in healthy IPV-primed adults aged 18–45 in Belgium. Day-1 randomization to one of three Day-1 doses — IPV alone (n=30), IPV+dmLT (n=30), or open-label bOPV (n=20). All three groups received a bOPV challenge dose on Day 29. IPV and IPV+dmLT arms were blinded; bOPV arm was open-label. Solicited/unsolicited AEs collected via diary.
